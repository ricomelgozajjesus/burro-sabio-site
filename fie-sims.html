Our Simulation Philosophy

Richard Feynman's purported statement, "Calculus is the language of God," resonates deeply with the idea that mathematics, particularly calculus, provides a framework for understanding and describing the natural world. Calculus allows us to model change, continuity, and the interconnectedness of phenomena, from the motion of celestial bodies to the flow of water and even the growth of populations. If true, Feynman's sentiment reflects his awe at how mathematics unlocks the intricate patterns and mechanisms underlying reality. With powerful computers, simulations act as an extension of our ability to "speak" this divine language. By encoding natural laws into algorithms, we not only replicate but also explore the dynamics of complex systems in ways that were once unimaginable. This computational capability is an affirmation of how far we've come in understanding nature through mathematics.

At SimUMSNH, our approach to decision-making is deeply rooted in a robust simulation philosophy: we simulate a wide range of potential scenarios to explore the full spectrum of possibilities. From these simulations, our team meticulously reviews and selects the most attractive options based on defined criteria, ultimately refining them into the best proposed solutions.
